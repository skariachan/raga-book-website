MŌHANAM

Pentatonic rāga of brightness and purity

Rāga Structure

Ārohaṇa: S R₂ G₃ P D₂ S

Avarohaṇa: S D₂ P G₃ R₂ S

Jāti: Audava–Audava

Varjya Swaras: M, N

Rāgam Mōhanam with C as Shadjam

Introduction To Ragam

Mōhanam is a pentatonic (audava) rāga in Carnatic music and is traditionally classified as a janya of Harikāmbhōji (28th Melakarta). Some scholars also associate it with Mechakalyāṇi or Śaṅkarābharaṇam based on its rāga lakṣaṇa. In Hindustani music, its equivalent is Bhūp (also called Bhūpālī). Mōhanam is one of the most widely used pentatonic scales in the world and appears prominently in East and Southeast Asian musical traditions, including those of China and Japan.

Rāga Lakṣaṇam

Mōhanam derives its beauty from simplicity and symmetry. With only five swaras, it creates a bright and expansive soundscape.

Bhāva

Joyful, auspicious, and calm. Mōhanam rewards restraint and clarity more than speed.

Personal Notes

Responds well when the mind is calm. Over-speeding reduces its depth.
